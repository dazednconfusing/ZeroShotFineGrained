0 the macgillivray's warbler (geothlypis tolmiei) is a small species of new world warbler .
1  these birds are sluggish and heavy warblers with short tails , preferring to spend most of their time on , or near the ground , except when singing .
2 the macgillivray's warbler was named by john james audubon in honor of scottish ornithologist william macgillivray , although the proper credit to its discovery goes to john kirk townsend .
3  the scientific name "tolmiei" was given in honor of william fraser tolmie .
4 adult macgillivray's warblers will grow to be approximately 13 cm {5 to 6 inches} in length .
5  they are an olive-green color on  their upperparts and dull yellow below .
6  males have black heads and breasts , while females and immature birds have drab light gray heads and white eye-rings .
7  macgillivray's warblers are almost identical to their eastern counterparts , the mourning warblers , the only true difference between the two species being the mourning warbler's lack of an eye-ring .
8 macgillivray's warblers are migratory and spend their summers in temporate forests located in the western united states , and in boreal forests of west canada .
9  in autumn , these birds will migrate back to central america , where they will stay in temperate shrublands for the winter .

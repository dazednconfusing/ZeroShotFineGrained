0 cassin's kingbird (tyrannus vociferans) is a large tyrant flycatcher native to western north america .
1  the name of this bird commemorates the american ornithologist john cassin .
2 ==description==adults have a gray head with slightly darker cheeks; a dark unforked tail with a buffy fringe and gray-olive underparts .
3  they have a pale throat and deep yellow lower breast .
4 juveniles are duller and have pale edges on their wings .
5 ===similar species===cassin's kingbird and the western kingbird are similar in appearance .
6  cassin's is a little larger than the western and the upper parts are a darker gray than the western .
7  the most distinctive difference between the birds is that the cassin's has a thin white strip along lower edge of the tail feathers and the western has a thin white strip that runs along the edge of the tail feathers .
8  this difference can be seen in the image on the left .
9 ==distribution and habitat==in the summer , these birds can be found in california and from montana to utah , along the eastern rocky mountains .
10  their habitat includes rangelands and savannas .
11 these birds migrate to their winter quarters between southern california and northern central america .
12  they are permanent residents in south-central mexico , and their main wintering ranges are west of the gulf of california on baja california sur , and east of the sea on the mainland of western mexico .
13 ==behavior==they build a bulky nest on a horizontal tree limb in mid-story or the canopy of trees .
14  the three to five spotted white eggs have an incubation period of 18 to 19 days .
15 the cassin's kingbird primarily feeds on insects it preys upon from high perches by hawking .
16  it also eats berries and fruits in lesser quantities .
17 the call is a high-pitched shorter followed by a longer chirp , sounding like chi-beer .
18 ===mating ritual===in early spring , presumably after having chosen (or shown up with) their mate , they launch into a peculiar dance .
19  with excited high-pitched calls , they hover in unison , wings outstretched , over a favorite perch .
20  this dance takes place several times a day over several days , over several separate sites in an area covering {convert|2|to|3|acre|m2} .
21  the sites chosen for the dance appear to be the same sites used as hunting perches during the spring and summer .

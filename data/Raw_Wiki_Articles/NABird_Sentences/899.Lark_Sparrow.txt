0 the lark sparrow (chondestes grammacus) is a fairly large american sparrow .
1  it is the only member of the genus chondestes .
2 ==description and habits==this passerine bird breeds in southern canada , much of the united states , and northern mexico .
3  it is much less common in the east , where its range is contracting .
4  the populations in mexico and adjacent states of the united states are resident , but other birds are migratory , wintering in the southern united states , mexico and south to guatemala .
5 it is a very rare vagrant to western europe , with two accepted records in great britain in 1981 and 1991.the lark sparrow is distinctive .
6  adults have a typically sparrow-like dark-streaked brown back , and white underparts except for a dark central spot .
7  the cheeks and crown sides are chestnut , with white eyebrow and crown stripes .
8  the dark tail's corners are also white .
9  young lark sparrows are duller , and the underparts are streaked .
10 these birds forage on the ground or in low bushes .
11  they mainly eat seeds , but insects , including grasshoppers , are also eaten in the breeding season .
12  they form flocks on migration or in winter .
13 the breeding habitat is a variety of open habitats including grasslands and cultivation .
14  lark sparrows nest on the ground , laying three to six eggs in a grass cup nest sheltered by a clump of grass or other vegetation .
15  the eggs are white with black scrawling .
16 the song is two clear notes followed by a mixture of buzzes and trills .
17  the flight call is a thin sit .

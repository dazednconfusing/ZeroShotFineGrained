0 the lesser yellowlegs (tringa flavipes) is a medium-sized shorebird similar in appearance to the larger greater yellowlegs , although it is more closely related to the much larger willet; the fine , clear and dense pattern of the neck shown in breeding plumage indicates these species' actual relationships .
1 a medium-large shorebird , the lesser yellowlegs measures {convert|27|cm|in|abbr=on} .
2  the legs are yellow .
3  compared to the greater yellowlegs , the bill is shorter (visually about the same length as the head), slim , straight , and uniformly dark .
4  the breast is streaked and the flanks are finely marked with short bars .
5 their breeding habitat is clearings near ponds in the boreal forest region from alaska to quebec .
6  they nest on the ground , usually in open dry locations .
7 they migrate to the gulf coast of the united states and south to south america .
8 this species is a regular vagrant to western europe , and the odd bird has wintered in great britain .
9 these birds forage in shallow water , sometimes using their bill to stir up the water .
10  they mainly eat insects , small fish and crustaceans .
11 the call of this bird is softer than that of the greater yellowlegs .

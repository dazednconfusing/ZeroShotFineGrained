0 the least sandpiper (calidris minutilla) is the smallest shorebird .
1 ==description==this species has greenish legs and a short , thin , dark bill .
2  breeding adults are brown with dark brown streaks on top and white underneath .
3  they have a light line above the eye and a dark crown .
4  in winter , least sandpipers are grey above .
5  the juveniles are brightly patterned above with rufous colouration and white mantle stripes .
6 this bird can be difficult to distinguish from other similar tiny shorebirds; these are known collectively as "peeps" or "stints" .
7  in particular , least sandpiper is very similar to its asian counterpart , long-toed stint .
8  it differs from that species in its more compact , shorter-necked appearance , shorter toes , somewhat duller colours , and stronger wingbar .
9 ==breeding and migration==their breeding habitat is the northern north american continent on tundra or in bogs .
10  they nest on the ground near water .
11  the female lays four eggs in a shallow scrape lined with grass and moss .
12  both parents incubate; the female leaves before the young birds fledge and sometimes before the eggs hatch .
13  the young birds feed themselves and are able to fly within two weeks of birth .
14 they migrate in flocks to the southern united states , mexico , central america , the caribbean , and northern south america .
15  they occur as very rare vagrants in western europe .
16 ==feeding==these birds forage on mudflats , picking up food by sight , sometimes by probing .
17  they mainly eat small crustaceans , insects , and snails .

0 the cactus wren (campylorhynchus brunneicapillus) is a species of wren that is native to the southwestern united states southwards to central mexico .
1 ==description==the cactus wren is the largest north american wren , at {convert|18||23|cm|in|abbr=on} long .
2  unlike the smaller wrens , the cactus wren is easily seen .
3  it has the loud voice characteristic of wrens .
4  the cactus wren is much less shy than most of the family .
5  its marked white eyestripe , brown head , barred wings and tail , and spotted tail feathers make it easy to identify .
6  like most birds in its genus , it has a slightly curved bill .
7  there is little sexual dimorphism .
8 ==diet==the cactus wren primarily eats insects , including ants , beetles , grasshoppers , and wasps .
9  occasionally , it will take seeds , fruits , small reptiles and frogs .
10  foraging begins late in the morning and is versatile; the cactus wren will search under leaves and ground litter and overturn objects in search of insects , as well as feeding in the foliage and branches of larger vegetation .
11  increasing temperatures cause a shift in foraging behavior to shady and cooler microclimates , and activity slows during hot afternoon temperatures .

13 ==habitat==it is a bird of arid regions , and is often found around yucca , mesquite or saguaro; it nests in cactus plants , sometimes in a hole in a saguaro , sometimes where its nest will be protected by the prickly cactus spines of a cholla or leaves of a yucca .
14 ==behavior==the cactus wren forms permanent pair bonds , and the pairs defend a territory where they live all through the year .
15 in residential areas , cactus wrens are notorious for getting into mischief .
16  being curious birds , it is not uncommon for these wrens to be found flying about out-of-place in automobiles where the owner has left a window open or it may even enter homes with an open door or window and find itself trapped .
17 ==state bird==the cactus wren is the state bird of arizona .

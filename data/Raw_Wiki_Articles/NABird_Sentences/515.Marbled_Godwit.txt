0 the marbled godwit (limosa fedoa) is a large shorebird .
1  on average , it is the largest of the 4 species of godwit .
2  the total length is {convert|40|-|50|cm|in|abbr=on}, including a large bill of {convert|8|-|13|cm|in|abbr=on}, and wingspan is {convert|70|-|88|cm|in|abbr=on} .
3  body mass can vary from {convert|240|to|510|g|oz|abbr=on} .
4 adults have long blue-grey hairy legs and a very long pink bill with a slight upward curve and dark at the tip .
5  the long neck , breast and belly are pale brown with dark bars on the breast and flanks .
6  the back is mottled and dark .
7  they show cinnamon wing linings in flight .
8 their breeding habitat is the northern prairies of western canada-(canadian prairies), and the north central great plains , united states near marshes or ponds .
9  they nest on the ground , usually in short grass .
10 in autumn , they migrate in flocks to the coasts of california , the gulf of mexico , mexico and south america .
11 these birds forage by probing on mudflats , in marshes , or at the beach (see picture below) .
12  in short grass , they may pick up insects by sight .
13  they mainly eat insects and crustaceans , but also eat parts of aquatic plants .
14 their numbers were reduced by hunting at the end of the 19th century .
15  although they had recovered somewhat since that time , their population has declined in recent times as suitable habitat is used for farming .

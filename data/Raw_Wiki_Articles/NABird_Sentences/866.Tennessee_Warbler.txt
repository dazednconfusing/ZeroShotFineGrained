0 the tennessee warbler (oreothlypis peregrina) is a new world warbler that breeds in eastern north america and winters in southern central america and northern south america .

2  the breeding male has olive back , shoulders , rump and vent .
3  the flight feathers are brownish-black .
4  it has a slate gray neck , crown and eyeline .
5  the underside is a gray-white .
6  the female is similar to the male , but is much duller and has a greener tinge to the underside .
7  the tennessee warbler has long wings , short tail and a thin , pointy bill .
8  juveniles and first-year birds are quite similar to the female .
9  tennessee warblers resemble female black-throated blue warblers .
10  the only difference is that the black-throated blue has a darker cheek and two white wing spots .
11 this bird can be confused with the red-eyed vireo , which is larger , moves more deliberately and sings almost constantly .
12  the orange-crowned warbler can also look similar , but lacks the white eyebrow , is greyer-brown above and has yellow undertail coverts .
13 the song has three parts , which can be repeated endlessly: tecky tecky tecky tick tick tick tick tyew!tyew!tyew!tyew! it's call is a sharp tyick .
14  the flight call is a buzzy zzee .
15 == distribution ==it breeds from the adirondack mountains in new york through northern vermont , new hampshire , and maine north and west throughout much of canada .
16  also found breeding in northeast minnesota and northern upper peninsula of michigan .
17  it is migratory , wintering in southern central america and northern colombia and venezuela , with a few stragglers going as far south as ecuador .
18  it is a very rare vagrant to western europe .
19  this bird was named from a specimen collected in tennessee where it may appear during migration .
20 == ecology and behavior ==the tennessee warbler feeds mainly on insects and prefers the spruce budworm .
21  this species fluctuates in population with the quantity of the worms .
22  it also likes flower nectar , fruit and some seeds .
23 this warbler , like most others , is nervous and quick while foraging .
24  it creeps along branches and is found at all levels .
25  it is solitary while nesting , but forms mixed flocks after breeding .
26 the tennessee warbler prefers coniferous forests , mixed conifer-deciduous forests , early successional woodlands and boreal bogs .
27  they make a cup shaped nest made of dried grasses and moss lined with finer grasses , stems and hair .
28  the nest can be placed on the ground or above a bog in moss or in the base of a shrub .
29  the nest is built by the female and she lays 47 white eggs with brown splotches on them .

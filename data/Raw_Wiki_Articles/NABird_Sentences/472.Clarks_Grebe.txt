0 clark's grebe (aechmophorus clarkii) is a north american species in the grebe family .
1  until the 1980s , it was thought to be a pale morph of the western grebe , which it resembles in size , range , and behavior .
2  intermediates between the two species are known .
3 the "clark" of its common nameand its specific epithet "clarkii"honor john henry clark , a 19th-century american surveyor who was also a naturalist and collector .
4   the genus name aechmophorus comes from the ancient greek words "aichme", meaning spear , and "phoros", meaning someone who bears things around; it refers to the bird's long , daggerlike beak .
5 this species nests on large inland lakes in western north america and migrates to the pacific coast in winter .
6  it maintains local populations year-round in california , nevada , arizona-(the lower colorado river valley), and central mexico .
7  it feeds by diving for carp , herring , mollusks , crabs , and salamanders .
8 it performs the same elaborate courtship display as the western grebe .
9 ==description==clark's grebe is black-and-white and has long slender neck .
10  it ranges in size from {convert|22|-|29|in|cm} .
11  among its distinguishing features is its bill , which is slightly upturned and bright yellow , whereas the western grebe's bill is straight and greenish-yellow .
12  it shows white around its eyes , whereas black appears around the eyes of the western grebe .
13  the downy young are white , not gray .

0 ==description==abert's towhees are recognized by their relatively long tails , dark faces , and overall brown plumage .
1  they are related to sparrows and juncos but are more similar to thrashers in appearance .
2  they can be confused with california towhees , but their dark faces are distinct , and the range of these species only slightly overlaps .
3  the abert's towhee is the longest species in the diverse emberizidae at {convert|21|to|25|cm|in|abbr=on} long , but its length is boasted by a relatively long tail , at {convert|10|to|12|cm|in|abbr=on} in length .
4  males weigh from {convert|40|-|54.1|g|oz|abbr=on}, with an average of {convert|47.1|g|oz|abbr=on}, while females weigh from {convert|39.5|-|51|g|oz|abbr=on}, with an average of {convert|44.8|g|oz|abbr=on} .
5  in terms of weight , it is outbulked by several other towhee species .
6  among standard measurements , the wing chord is {convert|8.2|to|9.7|cm|in|abbr=on}, the bill is {convert|1.5|to|1.6|cm|in|abbr=on} and the tarsus is {convert|2.6|to|2.9|cm|in|abbr=on} .
7 ==habitat==this bird is common in brushy riparian habitats in the lower sonoran desert zone but may require some effort to see as it prefers to stay well-hidden under bushes .
8  though threatened by cowbird nest parasitism and habitat loss , it has successfully colonized suburban environments in the phoenix , arizona metropolitan area and may be fairly easily seen on the campus of arizona state university .
9  despite its limited range , it is classified as a species of least concern in the iucn redlist , and there has been some range expansion along the santa cruz river as well as in oak creek canyon near sedona .
10 ==feeding==these birds are often seen foraging among dense brush for seeds .
11  like other towhees , they scratch at the ground in a manner similar to quail , and will sometimes dig up and eat grubs .
12  they can be attracted to feeders by providing cracked corn on the ground .

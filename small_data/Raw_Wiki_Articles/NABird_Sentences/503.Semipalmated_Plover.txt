0 the semipalmated plover (charadrius semipalmatus) is a small plover .
1 ==description==this species weighs {convert|22|-|63|g|oz|abbr=on} and measures {convert|14|-|20|cm|in|abbr=on} in length and {convert|35|-|56|cm|in|abbr=on} across the wings .
2  adults have a grey-brown back and wings , a white belly , and a white breast with one black neckband .
3  they have a brown cap , a white forehead , a black mask around the eyes and a short orange and black bill .
4 ==habitat==their breeding habitat is open ground on beaches or flats across northern canada and alaska .
5  they nest on the ground in an open area with little or no plant growth .
6 they are migratory and winter in coastal areas of the southern united states , the caribbean and much of south america .
7  they are extremely rare vagrants to western europe , and have been found in tierra del fuego and the isles of scilly .
8  their true status may be obscured by the difficulty in identifying them from the very similar ringed plover of eurasia , of which it was formerly considered a subspecies .
9 ==behavior==semipalmated plovers forage for food on beaches , tidal flats and fields , usually by sight .
10  they eat insects , crustaceans and worms .
11  this bird resembles the killdeer but is much smaller and has only one band .
12  the term "semipalmated" refers to its partly webbed feet .
13  since the semipalmated plover nests on the ground , it uses a "broken-wing" display to lure intruders away from the nest , in a display similar to the related killdeer .

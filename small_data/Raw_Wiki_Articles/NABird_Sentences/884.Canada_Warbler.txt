0 the canada warbler (cardellina canadensis) is a small {convert|13|cm|in|abbr=on} long songbird of the new world warbler family .
1 ==description==these birds have yellow underparts , blue-grey upperparts and pink legs; they also have yellow eye-rings and thin , pointed bills .
2  adult males have black foreheads and black necklaces .
3  females and immatures have faint grey necklaces .
4  they have yellow "spectacles" round the eyes .
5 the canada warbler is the host to the parasite apororhynchus amphistomi .
6 ==ecology=====breeding===they breed generally in dense secondary growth forests , red maple swamps or high elevation alpine forests .
7  these forests are located across canada , east of the rockies , and in the eastern united states .
8  the nests are shaped like open cups and are placed on the ground in a damp , heavily wooded location , generally characterized by a sphagnum hummock , tree stumps or other woody debris .
9  the female lays four to five eggs and incubates for about 12 days .
10  the chicks remain in the nest for about 10 days after hatching and are dependent on their parents for two to three weeks after they leave the nest .
11 these birds migrate to northern south america , and are very rare vagrants to western europe .
12 ===feeding===they forage actively in vegetation or on the ground , and they often catch insects in flight .
13  these birds mainly eat insects .
14  they forage in flocks in their winter habitat .
15 ===song===the song of this bird is loud and highly variable , resembling chip chewy sweet dichetty .
16  their calls are low chup's .
17 ===status===canada warblers' numbers have declined due to loss of suitable habitat and the species has been assessed as "threatened" by the committee on the status of endangered wildlife in canada .
18  the iucn , however , ranks the canada warbler as a species of least concern .
19 the canadian population , which accounts for 85% of the global population , is estimated at roughly 2.7 million individuals .
20 the canada warbler is protected at the federal level in both canada and the united states .
21 canada warblers have been seen twice in europe .

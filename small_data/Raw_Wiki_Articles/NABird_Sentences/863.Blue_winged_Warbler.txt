3 ==taxonomy==the common name blue-winged warbler refers to the bluish-gray color of the wings that contrast with the bright yellow body of the male .
4  the name of the genus vermivora means "worm-eating" .
5  the genus used to include nine other new world warblers but now includes only the golden-winged warbler and bachman's warbler which is believed to be extinct .
6 the blue-winged warbler was one of the many species originally described by linnaeus in his 18th-century work , systema naturae , though the scientific name has changed several times .
7  the species epithet pinus was given by linnaeus in 1766 but was a mistake as the original description of the species was actually based on illustrations of "pine creepers" drawn by others .
8  the drawings depicted two different species , what we now call a pine warbler and blue-winged warbler .
9  in 2010 the blue-winged warbler's scientific name was changed by the american ornithologists union to correct the error .
10  pine warblers retained the species name pinus but the species epithet for blue-winged warbler was changed to cyanoptera .
11 ===hybridization with golden-winged warbler===this species forms two distinctive hybrids with golden-winged warbler where their ranges overlap in the great lakes and new england area .
12  the more common and genetically dominant brewster's warbler is gray above and whitish (male) or yellow (female) below .
13  it has a black eye stripe and two white wing bars .
14  the rarer recessive lawrence's warbler has a male plumage which is green and yellow above and yellow below , with white wing bars and the same face pattern as male golden-winged .
15  the female is gray above and whitish below with two yellow wing bars and the same face pattern as female golden-winged .

0 the ross's goose (chen rossii or anser rossii) is a north american species of goose .
1 the american ornithologists' union places this species and the other two "white" geese in the genus chen rather than the more traditional "grey" goose genus anser .
2 this goose breeds in northern canada , mainly in the queen maud gulf migratory bird sanctuary , and winters much further south in the continent in the southern united states and occasionally northern mexico .
3 the plumage of this species is white except for black wing tips .
4  it is similar in appearance to a white-phase snow goose but approximately 40% smaller .
5  other differences from the snow goose are that the bill is smaller in proportion to its body and lacks "black lips" .
6  the dark phase is extremely rare .
7 the ross's goose is a rare vagrant to western europe , but it is commonly kept in wildfowl collections and so the true frequency of wild birds is hard to ascertain .
8  escaped or feral specimens are encountered frequently , usually in the company of other feral geese such as canada goose , greylag goose and barnacle goose .

10 this species is named in honor of bernard r .

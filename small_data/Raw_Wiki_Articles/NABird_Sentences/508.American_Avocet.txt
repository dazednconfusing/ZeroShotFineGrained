0 the american avocet (recurvirostra americana) is a large wader in the avocet and stilt family , recurvirostridae .
1 ==description==this avocet has long , thin , gray legs , giving it its colloquial name , blue shanks .
2  the plumage is black and white on the back with white on the underbelly .
3  the neck and head are cinnamon colored in the summer and gray in the winter .
4  the long , thin bill is upturned at the end .
5  the adult bird measures {convert|40|-|51|cm|in|abbr=on} in length , {convert|68|-|76|cm|in|abbr=on} and {convert|275|-|420|g|oz|abbr=on} in weight .
6 ==breeding and habits==the breeding habitat is marshes , beaches , prairie ponds , and shallow lakes in the mid-west as far north as southern alberta , saskatchewan , and manitoba , and on the pacific coast of north america .
7  american avocets form breeding colonies numbering dozens of pairs .
8  when breeding is over the birds gather in large flocks , sometimes including hundreds of birds .
9  nesting occurs near water , usually on small islands or boggy shorelines where access by predators is difficult .
10  the female lays four eggs in a saucer-shaped nest , and both sexes take turns incubating them .
11  upon hatching , the chicks feed themselves; they are never fed by their parents .
12 this species is migratory , and mostly winters on the southern atlantic and pacific coasts of mexico and the united states .
13 the american avocet forages in shallow water or on mud flats , often sweeping its bill from side to side in water as it seeks its crustacean and insect prey .
